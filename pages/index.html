LiveWires

technical activity holiday for 12–15s

28th July–4th August • Salisbury, UK

How to Book

Start your summer learning more about Jesus and technology.

Choose coding, electronics, music or video

Build a technical project from scratch, with the tools used by professionals.

If you’ve never done this before, don’t worry: we’ll teach you everything you need to know. And you’ll take home what you’ve made to show off.

Is there more to life than tech?

Dig into the Bible through short talks, chatting with new friends, and praying together. Ask questions about the Christian faith, and discover what really matters.

This year we’ll be taking you into Paul’s letter to the Ephesians, seeing more of God’s plan and purpose for his church.

Take a break

We take time away from the computers every afternoon: jump in the pool, or chat by the sofa. You’ll end the day in a silly game with your dorm mates. And there’s the day out off-site…

Try something new

We’ll teach you everything you need, and you’ll take home what you make to show off.

There's a lot to choose from, but you’ll only have time for two or three activities.

Stop Motion

Create a short animation frame-by-frame using Lego, Plasticine, a camera, and a Mac.

Video Production

Shoot and edit a short film or trailer, from storyboarding through to post-production.

Recording Studio

Record, edit, and master a podcast or song with professional gear.

Electronics

Pick up a soldering iron and build a cool gadget. Choose between a guitar pedal, an escaping robot, an LED cube, and more.

3D Modelling

Sculpt and animate a virtual model from your imagination using Blender.

Coding

Write your own mobile game or app using Javascript. Or learn HTML or CSS to build a website.

Meet the team

LiveWires is run by a team of about 30 volunteers, including the overall leaders Steven and Roger.

Steven

Steven’s day job is designing computer systems for BBC Media Action. At LiveWires, Steven can often be found trying to run extra network cables around the building.

He lives near Oxford with his wife Karen and young sons Joshua and Reuben. They enjoy growing food in their garden and allotment.

Roger

Roger is an electronic engineer specialising in aircraft ejector seats. During LiveWires, you’re likely to find him peering at circuit boards and diagrams.

He lives in Uxbridge with his wife Nicola and daughter Rachel. In his spare time he’s a keen photographer!

Location

LiveWires takes place at Chafyn Grove School in Salisbury.

It’s an amazing space with great facilities:

  • a heated swimming pool
  • tennis courts and a playing field
  • six person dorms for sleeping
  • rooms we rig up for all the technical activities

How to book

LiveWires costs £287 for the week, which includes activities, food, accommodation, and the day out. Electronics projects cost a little extra.

You can book on Scripture Union’s website. SU have been running summer holidays for over a hundred years.
